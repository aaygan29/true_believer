# Conference venues: fit, status, and honest assessment

Aayush Gandhi. No em dashes. Which conferences fit this paper, which do not, and what is prepared.
Journal submission (Computational Brain & Behavior) is separate, under `../journals/`; it is submitted
and at Technical Check as of 2 Jul 2026.

## Prepared and ready

### EMNLP 2026 (main conference) - the best conference fit
- Folder: `emnlp2026/`. Anonymized, ACL-format LaTeX (`emnlp_paper.tex` + `refs.bib` + `acl.sty` +
  `acl_natbib.bst`), compiles to a 4-page double-blind PDF (`emnlp_paper.pdf`).
- Why it fits: computational persuasion and computational social science are active at EMNLP; the two
  text arms (ChangeMyView, Persuasion-for-Good) are squarely NLP. The paper is anonymized (no author,
  no repository or OSF links, code release deferred to acceptance).
- Deadline: direct submission 15 July 2026 (AoE), or via ARR. Needs an OpenReview account (yours) and the
  final submit is yours.
- Honest caveat: EMNLP is selective and the effects are modest; reviewers will push on the null pooled
  increment and the text-proxy framing. The within-pair result and the cross-domain generalization are
  the strongest parts to foreground for this venue.

## Plausible but not prepared (need a reformat or a later deadline)

### NeurIPS 2026 workshops
- The main track deadline has passed. Workshops (announced late summer, deadlines typically Aug-Sep) on
  AI safety, computational social science, or ML-for-neuroscience would fit the proof-of-risk framing and
  the neural arm. Reformat to the specific workshop template when calls open. Folder placeholder:
  `neurips_workshops/`.

### ICWSM 2027 (Web and Social Media)
- The ChangeMyView lineage (Tan et al. 2016) lives here, so the text arms fit well. Deadlines are usually
  mid-2026 for the 2027 cycle; confirm on the ICWSM site. Would use their AAAI-style template.

### SaTML (Secure and Trustworthy ML)
- The cognitive-security / proof-of-risk framing fits SaTML's remit. Good home if the security angle is
  foregrounded over the NLP angle.

## Poor fit (do not force)

### MLCB (Machine Learning in Computational Biology)
- MLCB is genomics, proteomics, and molecular computational biology. This belief/persuasion/neuro paper
  does not fit its scope, and submitting it there would read as off-topic. The author's separate TRIBE /
  neuroforecasting work is the appropriate MLCB-adjacent submission, not this one. Recommend not
  submitting this paper to MLCB.

### CogSci 2026
- Would have been a strong fit (formal model plus interdisciplinary), but the deadline (Feb 2026) has
  passed. CogSci 2027 is a future option; watch for the call.

## Recommendation

Submit the journal version to Computational Brain & Behavior (done). Submit the anonymized EMNLP version
before 15 July 2026 if you want a fast conference venue, foregrounding the within-pair and cross-domain
results. Hold the NeurIPS-workshop and ICWSM options for their next open calls. Skip MLCB for this paper.
